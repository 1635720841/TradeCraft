## What Is Laser Gum Contouring?

Laser gum contouring is a dental procedure that reshapes soft tissue around the teeth. Dentists use dental lasers and laser technology to precisely adjust tissue, which may reduce bleeding and support smoother healing.

Laser gum contouring in SoHo may help people who want a nearby option for cosmetic reshaping without a long commute across Manhattan.

## When This Treatment Helps

This procedure may help with gummy smiles, short-looking teeth, or uneven tissue around the teeth. Small changes to the gum line can make the smile look more balanced when the concern comes from excess coverage.

Laser dentistry can also support oral health in some cases, but cosmetic contouring is not the same as treatment for gum disease, gum recession, or procedures like a gum graft. Active inflammation should be treated first to reduce the risk of poor healing or unstable long-term results.

## How Treatment Works

Before any reshaping, a dentist or periodontist should evaluate the teeth, bite, tissue levels, bone support, and signs of inflammation, especially if you have a swollen gum around one tooth. This helps confirm whether the concern comes from excess tissue, tooth position, altered eruption, inflammation, or lip movement.

If laser reshaping is appropriate, the provider usually numbs the area, outlines the planned tissue changes, and removes small amounts of soft tissue with a dental laser. The goal is to expose a more balanced amount of tooth structure while avoiding over-removal, sensitivity, or an uneven result.

For health-related cases, the process is different. Laser therapy may target inflamed or infected tissue around the teeth, but active gum disease often needs a broader periodontal treatment plan before any cosmetic changes are considered.

## Pain, Recovery, and Results

Most patients feel pressure rather than sharp pain because the area is numbed. Recovery time depends on how many teeth are treated and how much soft tissue is adjusted. Some people feel mild tenderness, swelling, or sensitivity for a few days, especially while brushing or eating.

Your dentist may suggest softer foods, careful cleaning, and avoiding anything that irritates the area while it heals. Results can be long-term when the tissue shape is stable, and the cause has been treated. Good oral hygiene helps protect the area and support better oral health after treatment.
